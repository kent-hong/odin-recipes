Beef Pho

Description

Vietnamese beef pho, known as "phở bò," is a traditional Vietnamese soup that has become a beloved dish worldwide. This aromatic and flavorful soup is made from a rich, slow-cooked broth that typically simmers for hours with a blend of spices like star anise, cinnamon, cloves, and cardamom. The base of the broth is usually beef bones, which impart a deep, savory flavor. Thinly sliced beef, often a mix of cuts like brisket, flank, and tenderloin, is added to the hot broth, cooking just enough to remain tender. Rice noodles form the base of the soup, soaking up the broth and making each bite deliciously satisfying.

What sets Vietnamese beef pho apart is its balance of flavors and the fresh garnishes that accompany it. The soup is traditionally served with a side of fresh herbs such as Thai basil, cilantro, and mint, along with bean sprouts, lime wedges, and slices of chili for a customizable experience. The combination of the hearty broth, tender beef, and fresh, crisp garnishes creates a dish that is both comforting and invigorating. Whether enjoyed as a breakfast staple or a hearty meal at any time of day, pho is a culinary experience that reflects the depth and diversity of Vietnamese cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 4 pounds beef soup bones (shank and knee)
  • 1 medium onion, unpeeled and cut in half
  • 5 slices fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 2 pods star anise
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 4 quarts water
  • 1 (8 ounce) package dried rice noodles
  • 1 1/2 pounds beef top sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon chopped green onion
  • 1 1/2 cups bean sprouts
  • 1 bunch Thai basil
  • 1 medium lime, cut into 4 wedges
  • 1/4 cup hoisin sauce (Optional)
  • 1/4 cup chile-garlic sauce (such as Sriracha) (Optional)

Steps

  1. Roast the beef bones and onions until the bones are browned and the onions are soft.
  2. Make the broth.
  3. Soak the rice noodles for an hour. Meanwhile, bring the broth to a simmer.
  4. Boil the noodles, then drain and add them to a bowl.
  5. Top with sirloin, cilantro, green onion, and broth. Garnish with your desired toppings.
