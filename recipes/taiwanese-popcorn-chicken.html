Taiwanese Popcorn Chicken

Description

Taiwanese popcorn chicken, or "yán sū jī," is a popular street food that has captured the hearts of food lovers around the world. These bite-sized pieces of chicken are marinated in a flavorful mix of soy sauce, garlic, and five-spice powder, giving them a savory and aromatic taste that’s unmistakably Taiwanese. After marinating, the chicken is coated in sweet potato starch, which gives it a unique, crispy texture when deep-fried. The result is a delightful contrast between the crunchy exterior and the tender, juicy chicken inside, making each bite irresistibly delicious.

What makes Taiwanese popcorn chicken stand out is the way it's served. Typically, it's sprinkled with a generous amount of fried basil leaves, adding a fragrant and slightly peppery note that complements the chicken's savory flavor. Often enjoyed as a snack or appetizer, it’s perfect for sharing with friends or savoring on your own. The combination of rich, savory seasoning, crispy texture, and the aromatic punch of basil makes Taiwanese popcorn chicken a beloved comfort food that’s both simple and deeply satisfying. Whether you're strolling through a night market in Taipei or enjoying it at home, this dish offers a taste of Taiwan’s vibrant street food culture.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds boneless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon five-spice powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper, or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup tempura batter mix, dry, or as needed
  • 2 cups peanut oil for frying, or as needed
  • 1 bunch Thai basil, chopped
  • 2 green onions, chopped

Steps

  1. Mix chicken, garlic, soy sauce, ginger, five-spice powder, white pepper, salt, vinegar, and cayenne pepper together in a large bowl until well combined. Let marinate for 10 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a deep-fryer or large saucepan to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
  3. Whisk egg in a small bowl until smooth. Place dry tempura mix into a second small bowl. Dip chicken pieces one at a time into egg, then dredge in tempura mix, shaking off any excess.
  4. Working in batches, lower chicken carefully into hot oil and fry until golden brown, 5 to 8 minutes. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Transfer chicken to a platter and garnish with basil leaves and green onions.
